<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='https://www.zsolt.blog/2021/05/Addicted-to-block-references.html'>Addicted to Block References<br>MS Office vs. Roam vs. Obsidian</a>
</h3>
</div>

<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'>
<span class='byline post-timestamp'>
<meta content='https://www.zsolt.blog/2021/05/Addicted-to-block-references.html'/>

<div class='container post-body entry-content' id='post-snippet-6153802766485992979'>
<div class='post-snippet snippet-container r-snippet-container'>
<div class='snippet-item r-snippetized'>
Setting the stage  I am leading a project to review my company&#8217;s business strategy. I want to identify projects for next year&#8217;s business plan and to create a strong link between the strategy and our projects. This will help in storytelling with the people delivering these projects and to the executives governing the strategy.  This exercise has put the gap between information and knowledge management tools I use in private and available at work in the spotlight. There are four capabilities I miss most at work: backlinks, block references, tags, and a query language to work with the information.
